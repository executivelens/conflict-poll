First, let's lay some foundational work about humans.

The CMH Framework
C

Cognition

Our brain has a capacity limit.

M

Memory

Not every memory is created equally.

H

Hormones

Hormones affect emotions, and emotions shape how we interact with surroundings.

A great example of how these three blocks work for you — or against you — is the Labeling Effect.

You are 3–5 labels in people's minds.

The CMH framework · in action

How your brain turns a person into a label

C

Conserve energy

Your brain burns ~20% of your energy. Survival means spending as little of it as possible.

H

Danger signal

Heavy mental effort is expensive — so a stress hormone fires to flag it as a threat.

M

Compress to a word

So your brain squeezes a person into one word. Repeat the pattern, and that label sticks.

Ten labels would blow the budget — so only the 3–5 most obvious survive. That's who you are in their mind.

Whatever 3–5 labels this group of people had in mind about you…

How to create that positive emotion that creates the deepest memory

Rule of thumb

Corporate is about creating the perception that you have everything under control.

You might be thinking…

What?!Why?!

Here's the why
The core purpose of a corporation is to conduct business to create long-term value. Historically and legally, this primarily meant generating wealth for shareholders.
If you own a company and want to sell it today, how much do you sell it for?
The price a buyer pays you = average profitability × # of years it can sustain that profitability (an estimate you and the buyer negotiate and agree on).
So you need to convince the buyer your company can create X years of profitability and generate even more profit per year — so you take the most $$$ home from the deal.

The challenge

Make current & future investors believe your company is worth their investment — so they don't sell ↓ and more buy in ↑.

What you do

Give them the perception of a bright future — that you'll keep generating profit for years to come.

Aka — you, the CEO, have everything under control.

What do you need your COO · CTO · CFO · CXO to tell you?

1

What happened this quarter — did we hit the goal we promised?

2

If not — why? And why are we confident we'll hit it this quarter?

3

More importantly — stories with data that make the company more profitable (or less unprofitable). Aka: things are under control — so you can sell them as your vision to investors.

Btw — no agency regulates the vision & strategy you tell investors. You just have to fulfill it… with time. That's called setting the right expectation.

Each level sells the one above a "we've got it under control" story — to sell upward, all the way to the investors.

Your boss's emotion & hormone life-cycle

Your boss has their goals. They want pay bump, promotion, more scope, blah blah blah. Their leadership team decides these. And we now know that your boss needs to give their leadership the perception that his/her team is under control and is going to have everything under control. But your boss needs data, story, narratives to tell their leadership this. That's where you come in. You will be delivering these messages.

NervousAdrenaline · Cortisol
Assuming you don't have any label on you now, your boss' default emotion is nervous. Adrenaline and Cortisol are released. Their heart races because of the uncertainty of how their leadership perceives you — a reflection of if your boss has everything under control.
The momentDeepest emotion
If you pay close attention, when you present/talk in meetings, your boss' attention is actually on their boss' reaction to you vs what you are presenting. This is precisely where the deepest emotions are formed.
RewardDopamine
If you handled well and their boss is happy, a dose of dopamine is released in your boss' brain. You will get a good label and vice versa… If you present when your skip level's leadership is present, your skip level's attention is on their boss. And same rule applies.
Repeat→ Brand
If you repeat this, your positive label will become stickier and stickier. A super duper sticky label is called brand. That's how you form your personal brand.

3 layers of narrative POV

How to create a narrative that has impact and influence

Company-interest-centric narrative
e.g. Talks around how to drive business forward about customer, cost, revenue, turnaround time, risk.
Reporting-chain interest-centric narrative
e.g. We need to push back this product feature because we don't have bandwidth to support.
Self-interest-centric narrative
e.g. I can't do this product feature because my plate is full.
Strategy

Narrative of creating positive emotions from your leadership

1

Know your self-interest, but never use self-interest centric narrative in public meetings. (EVER!)

2

Always word things using company-interest centric narrative.

3

When company-interest and reporting chain interest have conflict — always prioritize reporting chain interest BUT wrapped with a company interest coat.

4

Lastly, know when to voice your self-interest (promotion, pay bump, more scope) to your leadership in 1×1 meetings AFTER you've done #3 consistently, aka you have a set of established and good labels. See pre-recorded CAARD framework of how to word it tactically.
