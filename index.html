First, let's lay some foundational work about humans.

The CMH Framework
C

Cognition

Our brain has a capacity limit.

M

Memory

Not every memory is created equally.

H

Hormones

Hormones affect emotions, and emotions shape how we interact with surroundings.

A great example of how these three blocks work for you — or against you — is the Labeling Effect.

You are 3–5 labels in people's minds.

The CMH framework · in action

How your brain turns a person into a label

C

Conserve energy

Your brain burns ~20% of your energy. Survival means spending as little of it as possible.

H

Danger signal

Heavy mental effort is expensive — so a stress hormone fires to flag it as a threat.

M

Compress to a word

So your brain squeezes a person into one word. Repeat the pattern, and that label sticks.

Ten labels would blow the budget — so only the 3–5 most obvious survive. That's who you are in their mind.

Whatever 3–5 labels this group of people had in mind about you…

So how do I create the labels I want people to see me as?

The label formula
Label = Key Memory(make it deep) × Spaced Repetition(make it stick)
Four levels of memory depth

Sensory

You just saw or heard it.

A stranger on the street.

gone in a minute

Meaning

It means something to you.

A reminder to pay rent.

hours–days

Drama

Big sights or sounds hit hard.

A new movie, song, anime or game.

days–weeks

Emotion

It stirs real feeling — good or bad.

A relationship or experience.

weeks–months
